Avocado & Artichoke Pasta Salad

Taste of Home
Pasta
2 cups gemelli or spiral pasta uncooked
14 oz water-packed artichoke hearts drained and coarsley chopped we use hand-torn oil-packed artichoke hearts
2 plum tomatoes seeded and chopped we used a handful of BB's very small baby tomatoes
1 medium-ripe avocado peeled and cubed
0.25 cup romano cheese grated
Dressing
0.25 cup canola oil
2 tbsp lime juice
1.5 tsp lime zest
0.5 tsp black pepper freshly ground
  1. Cook pasta per package directions. Drain and rinse with cold water.
  3. In a small bowl, mix canola oil, lime juice, lime zest, salt, and pepper.
  4. Pour dressing mix over pasta and toss gently to combine.
  5. Refrigerate covered until serving.

Chicken Tortilla Soup with Sweet Potato

Kettle & Fire
Soup
2 corn tortillas cut into strips we skip this part or sometimes use tortilla chips
2 avocado or olive oil
1 onion diced Mercedes dislikes cooked onions, we cut it into fourths to make it easier to remove after cooking
1 garlic clove minced
1 sweet potato peeled and diced
2 tsp dried oregano
1 tsp ground cumin
1 tsp chili powder or group cayenne pepper
28 oz fire-roasted diced tomatoes
4 cups bone broth
1 zucchini diced
2 cup chicken cooked and shredded we use a rotisserie chicken but boneless, skinless chicken breasts work too
3 limes quartered
  1. Preheat oven to 375°F. Place tortilla strips on a baking sheet and transfer to oven. Cook until crispy and brown (8-10 minutes).
  2. Meanwhile add oil to a large pot over medium-high heat.
  3. Add onion to pot and cook until soft (4-6 minutes).
  4. Add sweet potato, oregano, cumin, and chili powder to pot. Cook until fragrant (~1 minute).
  5. Add tomatoes and broth to pot. Cook until flavors meld (10-12 minutes).
  6. Add zucchini and chicken to pot. Cook until zucchini is soft (10-12 minutes).
  7. Add salt to taste.
  8. Remove from heat and stir in lime juice to taste.
  9. To serve, add tortilla strips to bowls and pour over soup.
  10. Optionally add additional toppings like cilantro, radish, or yogurt.

Slow Cooker Pulled Chicken Sandwiches with Apple Cider Slaw

FitnessBlender
BBQ Chicken
2 cloves garlic peeled and chopped
1 yellow onion peeled and chopped as with above, we tend to cut it into bigger pieces to make it easier to remove afterward
0.5 cup natural ketchup
2 tbps apple cider vinegar
2 tbps honey or agave
2 tbps dijon mustard
1 tbsp soy sauce or coconut aminos
1 tsp smoked paprika
30 oz chicken quarters bone-in, skinless breasts or thighs can use jackfruit instead for a vegetarian modification
4-6 whole wheat burger buns split and toasted can use gluten free
Slaw
1 tbsp olive oil
2 tbsp apple cider vinegar
1 tbsp honey or agave
0.25 tsp celery seed we tend to skip since we never remember to buy
0.25 tsp salt
1 pinch black pepper
1.5 cups coleslaw mix green cabbage, red cabbage, carrots sometimes we merely julienne & chop carrots and leave out the cabbages
1 cup kale shredded
0.5 cup scallions chopped
  1. Set slow cooker or crock pot to high.
  2. Stir in onion, garlic, ketchup, vinegar, honey, mustard, soy sauce and paprika.
  3. Add in chicken pieces and cover with lid.
  4. Cook for 5-6 hours. If using jackfruit instead, only simmer for 2-3 hours.
  5. Remove chicken from the pot and remove the meat from the bones. Discard the bones.
  6. Return the chicken to the pot and pull into shreds using a fork or tongs.
  7. Season to taste with salt and keep warm until ready to serve.
  8. In a large bowl whisk together olive oil, vinegar, honey, celery seeds, salt and pepper until blended.
  9. Toss dressing with coleslaw mix, kale and scallions until coated.
  10. Top toasted buns with chicken and slaw before serving.

Instant Pot Ground Beef Chili

NomNomPaleo
Soup
1 tbsp avocado oil
1 large yellow onion diced
1 small red bell pepper diced
1 pinch kosher salt
2 tbsp tomato paste
4 cloves garlic minced
2 lbs ground beef 85% lean / 15% fat
3 tbsp chili powder
1 tbsp dried oregano
1 tbsp ground cumin
0.25 tsp cayenne pepper optional
14.5 oz fire-roasted diced tomatoes drained
0.5 cup chicken broth
2 tsp fish sauce
  1. Press the sauté button on the Instant Pot and wait for the metal insert to heat up. Once the insert is hot, add 1 tablespoon of fat and swirl it around.
  2. As soon as the oil is shimmering, add the onions and bell peppers with a sprinkle of salt.
  3. Cook for 3 minutes, stirring frequently, until the vegetables are softened.
  4. Stir in the tomato paste and minced garlic and cook until fragrant, about 30 seconds.
  5. Add in the ground beef, along with 1 teaspoon Diamond Crystal brand kosher salt. Brown the meat and break it up with a spatula.
  6. When most of the ground beef is no longer pink (5 to 7 minutes), stir in the chili powder, oregano, cumin, and cayenne pepper (optional). Combine well to distribute the fragrant spices.
  7. Toss in the drained diced tomatoes, broth, and fish sauce. Stir everything well, making sure the liquid reaches the bottom of the insert.
  8. Lock the lid and cook under high pressure for 15 minutes. (In other words, press the “Manual” or “Pressure Cook” button and set the time for 15 minutes under high pressure.)
  9. When the chili is finished cooking, release the pressure manually if you’re itching to eat it right away. Otherwise, you can let the pressure come down naturally and chow down when you’re ready.
  10. Taste the chili and adjust the seasoning if necessary. Sprinkle on some extra salt and stir in the apple cider vinegar, if desired. Taste again to see if you need to punch up the flavors.
  11. Ladle up the chili and serve with your favorite toppings, like sliced avocado, white onion, and cilantro!
  12. You can store the chili in the fridge for up to 4 days in the fridge or up to 6 months in the freezer.
